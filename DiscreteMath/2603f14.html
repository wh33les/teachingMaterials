      • Concepts:
        • § 6.3: Generalized Permutations and Combinations
          • # of permutations of n objects; t types of objects, e.g., "How many strings can be formed using the letters M I S S I S S I P P I ?"
          • vs. # of unordered k-element selections from a set X of t elements, with repetitions allowed, e.g., "How many monomials of degree d are there, using n variables?"
        • SKIP § 6.4
        • § 6.5: Introduction to Discrete Probability
          • experiment, event, sample space
          • computing it
        • § 6.6: Birthday Problem
        • § 6.7: Binomial Coefficients and Combinatorial Identities
          • "Determine the coefficient of 'blah' in the expansion of 'blerg'" -- What happens if 'blerg' is not a binomial?
          • State the Binomial Theorem
          • Pascal's Triangle
          • binomial expansions
          • proving combinatorial identities, via counting arguments or algebra
        • § 6.8: The Pigeonhole Principle
          • three forms given in the text -- understand why they are all the same and when each applies
          • strategy: In a given problem, find the "pigeon" and find the "pigeonhole". If it is not obvious, try one of the other stated forms of the Pigeonhole Principle.
          • Pay attention to the assigned problems.
        • § 7.2: Solving Recurrence Relations
          • quiz questions
          • 1st order: solving via iteration
          • understand initial conditions/order, linear, homogeneous, constant coefficients
          • 2nd order: compare to 2nd order recurrence relations in diffyQ
      • § 8.1: Intro to Graph Theory
        • definitions
        • how to show whether or not a graph is bipartite
        • connectedness of graphs
      • § 8.2: Paths and Cycles
        • difference between path, simple path, cycle/circuit, simple cycle, Euler cycle
        • When does an Euler cycle exist?
      • § 8.3: Hamiltonian Cycles
        • vs. Euler cycle
        • showing one doesn't exist
        • Traveling Salesperson: Hamiltonian cycle on a weighted graph
      • SKIP § 8.4
      • § 8.5: Representations of Graphs
        • adjacency matrices and their properties as they relate to graphs
        • back and forth between a graph and its adjacency matrix
        • What does the nth power of an adjacency matrix tell us?
        • incidence matrix: know the definition
      • § 8.6: Isomorphisms of Graphs
        • examples of invariants and proving something is an invariant
        • exhibiting an isomorphism -- What must be satisfied?
      • § 8.7: Planar Graphs
        • Euler formula and how it's used
        • Kuratowski's Theorem
        • series reduction, homeomorphic graphs
