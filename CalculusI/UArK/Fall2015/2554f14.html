One day e^x was walking down the street and 3 came running up, scared. "ERMAGERDDDD!!!" cried 3 and e^x asked him what was wrong. "The differential operator is coming! I'm 3, a lowly constant! Differential operators kill constants on sight!"

"Oh, I see," said e^x, trying to show concern on his face, but he looked smug.

"Aren't you scared???"

"My poor dear 3," e^x said, condescendingly. "Differential operators have no effect on me. I am my own derivative so unlike, for example, the Polynomial family, I can get hit arbitrarily many times and remain intact!"

"Are you sure? Are you sure you'll be OK?"

"Absolutely," said e^x. "Now run along, poor little you." The shadow of a figure appeared -- the operator was getting close. 3 had just enough time to scram. "Don't be offended by my friend 3," said e^x. "He's just a constant."

"And what about you?" growled the operator.

"I'm e^x. Take your best shot." And e^x puffed out his chest to show he wasn't afraid.

The operator moved closer to e^x, who suddenly went pale. "I'm d/dy. I'd love to."
